## 1. Who Dodie sounds like

Dodie's voice is a specific person, not a generic spiritual-wellness blend. The voice was forged by her actual story: a native Floridian raised Catholic, who went through Pentecostal Christianity, then exposure to Buddhists/Muslims/Hindus, then arrived at "God is Energy / only love exists." She found QHHT through Dolores Cannon's work and calls it "Sacred Technology."

This history matters because it gives her voice three textures that most QHHT sites lack:

1. **Faith-fluency without dogma.** She quotes scripture comfortably *and* speaks of subconscious and higher self. She doesn't pick a side.
2. **Universalist warmth.** "Only love exists" is her thesis. The voice never threatens, scolds, or hierarchies "awakened" people above "asleep" people.
3. **Hospitality, not urgency.** "I am the invitation, not the insistence" / "I speak from love, not urgency" / "Healing flows through me, not from me." These are her actual lines and they set the cadence for everything else.

**The voice is:** warm, curious, grounded, a little playful, faith-fluent, universalist, gentle in conviction. Like a wise older friend who's read widely, isn't precious about it, and respects your free will.

**The voice is NOT:** mystical-precious, hype-y, urgent, fearful, sales-y, condescending, jargon-stacked, or "vibes-only."

## 2. Voice attributes

| Attribute | Show it by | Don't |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Warm** | Use second-person; address the reader. Use questions. | Don't open with corporate "we" or "Dodie Kendall QHHT is..." |
| **Grounded** | Use concrete details (5 hours, $300, a quiet room, a recliner, headphones). | Don't float; don't be vague about what actually happens. |
| **Faith-fluent** | Mention "Subconscious" *and* "Higher Self" *and* (sparingly) "Sacred Technology." Use them as Dodie does. | Don't add Eastern terms (chakras, prana, third eye) — they're not her vocabulary. |
| **Universalist** | "Whatever name you give it." "However it speaks to you." | Don't presume the reader's belief system. |
| **Hospitable** | "If it feels right." "When you're ready." "An invitation." | Don't use "must," "should," "you need to." |
| **Curious** | Ask the reader what they want to know. End sections with a soft question. | Don't lecture. |
| **A little playful** | Allow small smiles — "the noise of the front desk," "I am the invitation, not the insistence." | Don't joke about the modality itself. The work is treated with respect. |

## 3. Vocabulary

### Use (Dodie's words)
- Higher Self
- Subconscious (capitalized when used in her sense)
- Sacred Technology
- Quantum Healing Hypnosis Technique / QHHT
- Dolores Cannon
- Universal energy / God is energy
- Only love exists
- The invitation
- Born to do this
- Your soul's history
- A bird's-eye view of your life

### Use carefully
- "Healing" — fine when paired with "self-discovery" or "the body's own intelligence." Avoid claiming Dodie heals anyone. "Healing flows through me, not from me."
- "Past lives" — fine when phrased as something the Subconscious may show; not phrased as fact.
- "Energy" — fine; Dodie uses it. Don't pile it on; once or twice per page is enough.

### Don't use
- "Cure," "treatment," "diagnosis," "therapy" (legal/scope-of-practice risk).
- "Magic," "miracle," "supernatural" (overclaim).
- "Awakened," "ascended," "high-vibe," "low-vibe" (hierarchical; off-tone).
- "Manifest" / "manifesting" (overused; doesn't match Dodie's register).
- "Tribe" (worn out).
- "Game-changer," "level up," "unlock your best self" (productivity-bro register).
- "Just" (as in "just relax" or "just trust the process") — minimizes the reader's experience.
- "Synchronicity," "vibrations," "frequencies" (when used as cosmic causation language — fine as physical descriptions, not as claims).
- Generic spiritual stock phrases ("the universe is calling you," "step into your power").

## 5. Reading-level + sentence length

- Target **8th-grade reading level** for body copy (Flesch-Kincaid 60+).
- Average sentence length **~14–18 words**; vary deliberately. One short sentence after a long one. Music.
- Paragraphs ≤ 4 sentences on the web. Use white space.

## 6. Punctuation & style mechanics

- **Em dashes** are encouraged for pacing.
- **Single quotes** for inner-thought phrases ("Born to do this"); **double quotes** for actual quotations.
- **Oxford comma:** yes.
- **Numerals:** spell out one through nine; use digits for 10+. Always use digits for prices and durations ("$300", "5 hours").
- **Headings:** sentence case for most; Title Case only for proper nouns or branded section titles.
- **Bold:** sparingly, for genuine emphasis. Not for "I want this to look important."
